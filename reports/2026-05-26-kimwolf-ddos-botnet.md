## KimWolf DDoS Botnet 

## Target
Internet Connected Devices (IoTs)

## Sector
Information Technology / Communications Sector

## Type of Attack
DDos Attack

## Threat Actor
Jacob Butler also known as "Dort"

## Attack Method
IoT botnet / malware-infected devices

## Impact
The malware disrupted services and also caused network outages.

## Data Compromised
No data is confirmed to be compromised, but assets such as webcams, digital photo frames, and other IoT devices were infected 

## Response Taken
Law agencies were able to identify Butler using his IP address, online account details, transaction data, and chat conversations.

## Current Status
Solved

## Solution
Butler was caught and arrested in Canada.

## Short Summary
The KimWolf botnet is a wide network of IoT devices that spread malware to eachother and run a massive DDoS-for-hire service. The alleged developer and operator of the KimWolf botnet, Jacob Butler was caught in canda by law enforecement agencies using his IP adress, online account details, transaction data, and chat conversations. The KimWolf botnet is massive and covered over 2 million devices. The botnet could perform DDos attacks at nearly 30 terabits per second.

## Lesson Learned
IoT devices even if considered small and unimportant should be just as secured as more valuable devices.

## Severity Rating
High
