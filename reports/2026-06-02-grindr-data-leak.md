# Grindr Data Leak

## Date
June 2, 2026

## Target
The Grindr app and its users

## Sector
Technology Sector

## Type of Attack
The type of attack used to find this data is still unclear, but the data revealed in the hack could be used in **Credential Stuffing** attacks.

## Threat Actor
Unknown as of right now.

## Attack Method
Although the attack method is unclear, data exposed can be used by adversaries to attempt credential stuffing attacks. A credential stuffing attack is when information from data leaks are reused in different websites to try and get access to user accounts that have the same login/password.

## Impact
The amount of data leaked has not been revealed by the attackers, but they are selling it for $400 which leads reaserchers to believe that the scope of the attack could be minor. But all 13.5 million users should stay alert for phishing emails and change passwords in different websites.

## Data Compromised
Wide range of data including full name, date of birth, usernames, encrypted password hashes, encrypted phone number hashes, geolocation data, account timestamps, and device information.

## Response Taken
Grindr has not yet commented on the attack but when they do I will update this report.

## Current Status
Ongoing

## Short Summary
Grindr is a widely-used dating app with a user-base of about 13.5 million users worldwide. A unknown hacker group listed user data from Grindr, including sensitive data like password hashes, for $400. This data can be used in order for adversaries to attempt credential stuffing attacks as well as phishing attacks. Grindr has yet to comment on their response of the attack, but the attack should not be taken lightly. All Grindr users should stay cautious and change passwords to accounts that have the same password as their Grindr account. Dating apps are a jackpot for hackers and with recent events like the ShinyHunters Bumbl hack, dating companies should take more secure measures in order to keep their users secure.

## Lesson Learned
Users should have different passwords in all of their online accounts.

## Severity Rating
High
